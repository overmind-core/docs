Overmind improves production AI agents by analyzing their code, capturing traces from every run, and driving autonomous, data-backed optimization that keeps only changes that measurably help. You register your agent, define a policy that describes correct behavior, and Overmind handles diagnosis, candidate code generation, scoring, and regression-safe acceptance — so you review a diff and a report, not every intermediate tweak.

## How the optimization loop works

After you register your agent and kick off an optimization run, Overmind executes a structured, repeating pipeline until it finds a measurably better version of your agent.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run your agent on a test dataset">
    Overmind executes your agent against every case in your training set and records a full trace of every LLM call, tool invocation, intermediate result, and final output.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Score outputs against your eval spec">
    Outputs are scored against an evaluation spec derived from your policy — checking structural correctness, value accuracy, cross-field consistency, tool usage, and optionally a policy-aware LLM-as-Judge.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Diagnose failures with a reasoning model">
    A strong reasoning model receives your agent code, per-case traces, scores, and your policy document. It identifies root causes and failure patterns — not just that a score was low, but exactly where and why things went wrong.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate candidate fixes">
    Multiple candidate code changes are generated, each targeting a different optimization area: tool descriptions, core logic, input handling, system prompt. A best-of-N approach maximizes the chances of finding a genuine improvement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept the best candidate or revert">
    Candidates are validated, re-scored on the full dataset, and accepted only if they beat the global best score and don't regress individual test cases. If no candidate improves things, the iteration is marked as a stall and optimization proceeds to the next cycle or stops cleanly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After several iterations you receive a measurably better agent, a readable report, and a diff showing exactly what changed.

## Who Overmind is for

<Note>
  Overmind is built for teams that already have, or are about to ship, **data-backed agents performing specific real-world jobs** — for example:

  - A **lead qualifier** scoring inbound sales inquiries
  - A **support triage router** classifying and drafting responses
  - A **contract clause extractor** working over long legal documents
  - A **clinical coder** converting encounter notes into ICD/CPT codes
  - An **AP invoice agent** making payment decisions with policy and fraud signals
  - An **on-call triage agent** investigating and routing incidents
  - A **returns concierge** applying policy and writing customer copy
  - A **research-brief multi-agent** producing marketing and SEO outputs

  If your agent is a chat toy, a single-shot prompt, or a one-off internal demo, Overmind is overkill. If your agent runs against real data, has a defined input/output contract, and you care about regression-safe improvement over weeks and months — that's exactly what Overmind is built for.
</Note>
