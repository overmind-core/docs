## What is Overmind?

Overmind helps you understand and improve the AI agents you run in production. You connect your agent and the data it produces, you decide what a good response looks like, and Overmind measures your agent against that bar and helps you raise it.

Everything happens in the [Overmind console](https://console.overmindlab.ai). You connect a code repository, Overmind reads it and builds a picture of your agent, and from there you can review its behavior, score it on real examples, and run optimizations that keep only the changes that help.

## Who is this for

Overmind is built for teams that already have an AI agent running, or close to it, and want to make it better with evidence instead of guesswork.

You will get the most out of Overmind if your agent lives in a GitHub repository, since Overmind reads that code to understand how the agent works. It also helps to have a sense of what a good response looks like for your use case, whether that is a correct answer, a safe one, or the right tool being called. If you send production traces to Overmind, the picture gets sharper, because the agent is then measured against situations it actually faced.

You do not need an evaluation harness, a labeling pipeline, or model-training infrastructure of your own. Overmind provides those parts. If you are still prototyping and have no agent yet, Overmind can still help once there is code to point it at.

## How Overmind helps you

- [Onboarding and Integrations](/onboarding/onboarding) is where you create your account and connect the tools you already use.
- [Repositories](/context/repositories) and [Agents](/context/agents) are where Overmind turns your code into agents you can inspect and work with.
- [Datasets and Traces](/context/datasets-and-traces) hold the real-world data and test sets that everything else is measured against.
- [Evaluation](/evaluation/eval-metrics) is where you define what "good" means, score your agent, and compare versions.
- [Optimizers](/evaluation/optimizers) propose and test improvements, and report the changes that move your scores.
- [Fine-tuning](/fine-tuning) trains a private model on your own data, with recommended models and settings. Available in early access.
- [Inference](/inference) serves your fine-tuned models and lets you try them in the Playground. Available in early access.

## How it works

You connect your agent and its data, you tell Overmind what a good response looks like, and Overmind tests and scores the agent for you. When you run an optimization, Overmind proposes changes, tries them on your data, and shows you the ones that improve your scores. You stay in control the whole way, and you can always see what changed and why.

## Where to start

If you are new, begin with [Onboarding and Integrations](/onboarding/onboarding) to set up your account and connect your tools.

To understand the building blocks, read [Repositories](/context/repositories), [Agents](/context/agents), and [Datasets and Traces](/context/datasets-and-traces).

When you are ready to measure and improve, see [Evaluation Metrics](/evaluation/eval-metrics) and [Optimizers](/evaluation/optimizers).
